My Music

Art can sometimes get awfully boring and tiring if you're sitting down for hours continuously. Music is a way to reduce stress and anxiety, and also makes art at lot more fun! Here are some of my favourite songs and artists that I recommend listening to.

Imase

Genre: J-Pop

Imase has been rapidly climbing up the music charts, especially with his hit song, "NIGHT DANCER". Despite being a 21 year old self-taught artist, he is extremely talented, only learning how to create music in just one year. I generally like songs that have a quick beat rather than calmer lofi songs, and I think Imase demonstrates that type of music perfectly.

TWICE

Genre: K-pop

TWICE has been my favourite music group for the past five years, and I've collected many of their albums and posters. Consisting of nine members, each member brings a unique voice and element to the table. They have several hit songs, such as "TT" and "What is Love?" that also helped them achieve SOTY (Song of The Year) award three years in a row and also making them million-album sellers. TWICE's songs all are upbeat and a nice groove to them, and I totally recommend listening to them when you have the chance. "Sandcastle" is my favourite - I can listen to that song for the rest of my life and I'll still enjoy it.

Mario Kart Theme Songs

Genre: Video Game Music

Having Mario Kart here seems pretty out of the blue, but there's something about Mario Music that makes it so unique. Mario Music is fun and playful, but also often extremely fast. I often use the music to get work done faster, but also while I paint since its fast and playful tempo makes it very addicting.


Correlation Between Music and Visual Arts

Believe it or not, music and visual arts have a very close relationship - they are both forms of art anyways. Music affects our subconscious mind, and depending on what you listen to, it can greatly impact the outcome of your painting. It has a strong effect on mark-making and colour choices that refect your mood. If you decide to listen to fast music (ex: Mario Kart music), it will naturall incite fast mark-making. This has been proven by many musicians, psychologists and artists.
