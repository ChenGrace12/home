History Of Art

Art has been around for thousands and thousands of years. Creating by humans, we focus on art for spiritual, narrative, philosophical, symbolic, conceptual, documentary, decorating, functional, and many, many other purposes. Visual art has progressed significantly through its aesthetic form. Forms of art include:


  • Cave Carvings
  • Statue Sculpting
  • Paint
  • Photography
  • Video Art
  • Architecture
  • Much more!

The history of art is often told based on the chronology of masterpieces that have been created in each era/civilization.

Timeline

Ancient Art

Ancient art refers to the many arts produced by old, advanced cultures and has created the foundation of art.

Renaissance

The Renaissance was a period in European history making the transition from the Middle Ages to modernity. Through this period, art has been used to help revive and surpass ideas and achievements of classical antiquity.

Baroque

Baroque art consists of visual design and architecture during the era in the history of Western art that coincides with the 17th century. Baroque art is stylistically complex and evokes emotional states by appealing dramatically to human senses.

Impressionism

Impressionism was an art movement that consisted of small, thin, brush strokes to depict unusual visual angles and a moment, especially in terms of shifting the effect of light and colour.

Post-Impressionism

Post-Impressionism was, obviously, after the Impressionism era. This movement applied the reaction against the naturalistic depiction of light and colour in Impressionism. It contained more symbolic, formala and structural content.

Expressionism

Expressionism refers to the art movement where the image of reality is distorted to enhance the artist's intentions and feelings. Artists were able to express their subjective emotions and ideas with artistic style.

Cubism

Cubism was a revolutionary art movement that incorporated different views of subjects into the same picture. Cubism is notably different from after arts because of the large use of geometric shapes and their depth.

Dada

Dada art was a turning point in art history. Starting from the WW1, these art pieces incorporated the negative reaction and harrors and folly of the war. Dada art was quite silly, irrational and absurd.

Pop-Art

Pop-art was heavily influenced by modernized, popular, mass culture in the 1950's. This included advertising, comic books, and mundane mass-produced objects.

Op-Art

Op-art, short for optical art, included many illusions, making art incredibly interesting to look at. They often incorporated art to give the viewer impressions of movement, hidden images, flashing and vibrating patterns, or swelling or warping.


The birth of modern art traces back to the Industrial Revolution. The rapid evolution of manufacturing, transportation, and technology began around the mid-18th century, affecting society's take at social, economic, and cultural conditions in life. Artists around this time began to experiment with new ways of seeing, and used fresh ideas about the nature of materials and functions of art.



My Art

Digital Art

What is it?

Digital art can be computer generated, scanned, or drawn using tables or a mouse. Ever since the 1990s, digital art became available after it was possible to download videos onto computers. People have been so used to creating art by pen and paint for centuries, so this was a real turning point for artists. They had the creative freedom that they never experienced before.


Digital art is still something I'm fairly new to, but so far it's been pretty fun. In some aspects, art is a lot easier, but there are a lot of new features that I'm new to and I haven't quite understood yet. However, I think it's one of my favourite forms of art yet. Here in the following pictures, I have fairly simple designs - a "sticker sheet", bunny cake, and a person holding a book.



Sketching

What is it?

Sketching is traditionally a rough drawing or painting in which artists use to note down their preliminary ideas. They require little to no precision, and are fairly quick. Sketches are used like a blueprint to portray the overall feeling and composition of a final piece.


Sketching is my favourite form of art - it's simple and fun. Sketching is a rough drawing, normally used for artists to note" down their ideas on paper. It can sometimes be messy and rough, but I often like the quick look of it. Sketches are usually less detailed than a study, but it's a great way for artists to visualize what they want to create.



Paint

What is it?

Painting is the practice of applying paint, pigment, color, or other mediums on a matrix or canvas. Through paint, you can create a variety of textures and tones to add depth to your creations. The birth of painting dates back to over 42,000 years ago and has been widely used with a variety of materials.


Whenever I pull out my canvas and my paints, I always underestimate how hard paint really is. I think I'm the least experienced when it comes to paint. I always find myself struggling to get the right precision and texture. I only paint when I have absolutely nothing else to do, since it's a slight hassle to pull out the many materials - and easel, the canvas, the paints, paint brushes, paint palettes, your water cup, and a a lot of paper. Despite the hard effort, it's often pretty therapeutic. I generally aim for more simplistic, cartoon designs so they can be easily hung up in my room.
