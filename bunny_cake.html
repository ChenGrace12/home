Bunny Cake



Step 1

In this piece, I used digital art. Before I even start thinking about colour, providing a sketch is crucial. Using the pastel pencil tool, I created a rough sketch of what I wanted to draw. It doesn't have to be perfect, just a general idea in terms of placement and what I want to include.


Step 2

After completing a rough sketch and having a general idea of where the objects are placed, I make a more defined outline. This makes colouring a lot easier, since I'll be able to have a fixed outline to guide me.


Step 3

Now, we can start with the colouring process. Using the chalk tool, I begin to colour ontop in the outline (I wanted to remove the bold black outline). As I colour, I experiment with colours and add them to my palette. For this piece, I used a more pastel feel.

Step 4

Now that I've completed the base colours, I add more variety in colour, especially for the strawberries, milk carton, and face. This is usually quite time consuming, since I am adding some more detail as well. For instance, using the blur feature on the strawberries to give it a blended look.

Step 5

After completing all the colour, here comes my favourite part: highlights. This process is quick, but gives your art immediately a lot more pop and dimension. And here's the final piece!
